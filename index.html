Detailed Dualist Questions (Version B)

1. Which of these best describes the mind’s relationship with the body?

2. Are thoughts and emotions literally identical to specific brain states—just described differently?

3. Do mental states (like pain or desire) play any role in changing physical behavior?

4. When you decide to lift your hand, is your conscious decision the cause of the movement—or just an accompanying feeling?

5. Imagine a person whose brain shows pain activity but who reports feeling nothing. How do you interpret that?

6. Picture someone saying “I believe lying is wrong” but consistently lies when it benefits them. What do you think?

Detailed Physicalist Questions (Version C)

1. Do you think consciousness is defined by what something is made of, or by what it does (how it processes information)?

2. Can a non‑biological system (like a computer or robot) have genuine thoughts and feelings if it behaves and processes information just like a human?

3. If someone says they believe it’s going to rain but never shows it in their behavior, do they really believe it?

4. Do you think terms like “belief,” “desire,” or “pain” will continue to be useful in future science?

5. How important is internal mental experience (like “feeling happy”) in understanding behavior?

6. How should we describe the mind in scientific terms?
